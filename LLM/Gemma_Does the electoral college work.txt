While such occurrences are undeniable, they fail to acknowledge the crucial role the Electoral College plays in ensuring that the President represents a broad coalition of states, not just densely populated urban areas. This system ensures that the voices of all states, regardless of size, are heard in the presidential election. The winner-take-all nature of the Electoral College in most states provides a clear and decisive outcome, avoiding the need for costly and potentially divisive recounts or run-off elections. This certainty of outcome is essential for maintaining public confidence in the electoral process and ensuring a smooth transition of power.
